Sanskrit Language Research

Integrating Sanskrit Language with Data Science: Machine Learning Approaches for Text Analysis

Independent Research

Download PDF or View Research Overview & Progress Report

Introduction

Sanskrit, often regarded as the "Mother of all Languages," is a classical language with unmatched grammatical precision, historical significance, and cultural richness. Despite its prominence in ancient Indian literature and philosophy, Sanskrit’s integration into modern computational linguistics and data science remains in its infancy. This research explores how machine learning (ML) and data science techniques can bridge this gap, offering novel ways to analyze texts, preserve cultural heritage, and develop advanced natural language processing (NLP) tools. Leveraging Sanskrit’s highly structured grammar system, this study aspires to make this ancient language accessible for global research and modern applications, ranging from digital education to programming language design.

Purpose and Scope

Linguistic Analysis:

Utilize ML to analyze Sanskrit texts for identifying linguistic patterns, such as morphological intricacies, syntactic structures, and semantic nuances.

Example: Computational analysis of Panini’s Ashtadhyayi grammar to understand its modern relevance.

Cultural Preservation:

Digitize Sanskrit manuscripts and apply ML to analyze and preserve these texts for future generations.

Example: Using OCR to convert ancient handwritten texts into machine-readable formats for digital archives.

Advancements in Natural Language Processing:

Develop state-of-the-art NLP tools tailored for Sanskrit, such as tokenizers, parsers, and machine translation systems.

Example: Building Sanskrit-English translation models for interdisciplinary research and global accessibility.

Programming Language Design:

Explore how Sanskrit’s rule-based grammatical framework can inspire structured programming languages or symbolic AI systems.

Example: Designing a logic-driven computational framework inspired by Panini’s sutras for modern programming tasks.
